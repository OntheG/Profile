Storyline

Gathur Delano Kafka is a reclusive thriller novelist whose works are known for their dense psychological tension and intricate mysteries. A gifted polyglot, he masters many languages such as English, German, Japanese, French, and others, using them instinctively both in daily life and in his writing. For Gathur, language is more than communication. Each language carries its own logic, emotion, and way of expressing fear. This belief shapes his novels, where foreign phrases, multilingual dialogue, and linguistic symbolism are woven into the narrative to create unease and hidden meaning. Readers admire his books for their intellectual depth, unaware that every word is rooted in fragments of his own fractured psyche.

After relentlessly pushing himself to complete his most complex thriller, Gathur becomes the victim of a severe accident that leaves him in critical condition. As his body lies motionless between life and death, his consciousness drifts instead of fading away. He opens his eyes to a world that feels hauntingly familiar, a dark city filled with distorted places, cryptic conversations, and an atmosphere thick with dread. Gradually, realization dawns on him that this world is not random. It is the very story he wrote, brought to life from his imagination, and somehow he has crossed into it at the moment his real body began to fail.

Inside the story, Gathur is stripped of his role as an author and forced to exist as part of the plot. The characters he once created now possess their own will, some treating him as a myth, others sensing him as a threat. The rules of the thriller govern everything, where secrets demand to be uncovered and mistakes are punished without mercy. His ability to understand multiple languages becomes his greatest weapon. Through scattered texts, coded warnings, and conversations that shift from one language to another, Gathur uncovers hidden layers of the narrative that only he can perceive. Each language reveals a different truth, guiding him deeper into the heart of the story and closer to the fears he tried to conceal through fiction.

As reality and fiction blur, Gathur is forced to confront the darkness embedded within his own writing. The story reflects his regrets, obsessions, and moral dilemmas, turning them into tangible threats. To escape, he must do the impossible by completing the story from within, not by writing words, but by making irreversible choices that shape its ending. Whether he will awaken from his critical state or remain forever imprisoned inside the thriller he created is uncertain, as the story continues to close in around him, demanding a conclusion that only he can deliver.
